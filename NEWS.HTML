TOP 5 TRANSFERS

1.Ibrahima Konate to Liverpool

Liverpool lost their English Premer League title to Manchester City last year due to serious injuries to their first choice center backs Virgil Van Dijk and Joe Gomez.
It forced Klopp to deploy Jordan Henderson and Fabinho at the heart of defence while the likes of Rhys Williams and Nathaniel Phillips were also given a shot.
The Reds have learned from their mistakes and added a strong defender to their squad in the form of Ibrahima Konate. The 22-year-old joins Liverpool for £36m from RB Leipzig ahead of next season.
The French center-back is expected to strike up a solid defensive partnership with Van Dijk and play a vital role for the Reds as they battle Man City for the PL title next year.

2.Jadon Sancho to Manchester United

The Devils have been on a hunt for a right-winger over the last few years. They have finally managed to end their wait and acquire the services of former Manchester City star Jadon Sancho. The ex-Dortmund star has been amongst the brightest players in the English football setup. He has delivered consistent performatives for the Bundesliga side last season and is expected to tear the Premier League apart. Ole Gunnar Solskjaer finished second in the league last season fellow neighbors and arch-rivals Manchester City. He will aim to break the final hurdle and emerge victorious in the Premier League 21-22 season.

3.Jack Grealish to Manchester City

Premier League champions Manchester City won the 20-21 Premier League season with great ease and comfort.
Pep Guardiola’s team cruised past their opponent’s like bread through butter winning the league with a 12-point gap. However, they failed to replicate their form in Europe losing to English rivals Chelsea in the Champions League final.

4.Cristian Romero to Totttenham Hotspur

Cristian Romero joins Spurs after helping Atalanta end the Serie A campaign with a third-place finish. He was also a part of the Argentina squad that won the Copa America in July earlier. The 23-year-old was named the ‘Best Defender’ in Serie A last season. Set to don the number four shirt, Spurs boss Nuno Espirito Santo will be hoping to see him help the London outfit in developing a challenge for the league next season.

5.Romelu Lukaku to Chelsea

The Blues managed to squeeze into the final four of the Premier League last season.
Despite signing Timo Werner from RB Leipzig, the London outfit visibly lacked a number 9 last season. Following the departure of Oliver Giroud and Tammy Abraham also likely on in way out, Chelsea has roped in Rome Lukaku. The 28-year-old Belgium international has joined the Stamford Bridge outfit from Inter Milan.
He was the Serie’ A outfit’s star and played a vital cog in their league win last season.
Heading into the upcoming campaign following a fantastic outing at Euro 2020, Thomas Tuhel will be hopeful of mounting a serious title challenge after adding the Belgian to his star-studded lineup.
